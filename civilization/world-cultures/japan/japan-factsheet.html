Overview

Japan is an island nation in East Asia, situated in the Pacific Ocean to the east of the Korean Peninsula and China. An archipelago of over 14,000 islands, it is one of the world's most densely populated countries and home to a highly urbanized society. Japan is a constitutional monarchy with a parliamentary government, and maintains one of the largest and most technologically advanced economies on Earth. Known for its blend of ancient traditions and cutting-edge innovation, Japan is a global leader in manufacturing, robotics, and popular culture.

Historical Milestones
660 BC
Traditional founding of Japan by Emperor Jimmu; beginning of the imperial line.
710
Nara established as Japan's first permanent capital; flourishing of Buddhist art and architecture.
1603
Tokugawa Ieyasu unifies Japan; Edo period begins, ushering in 250+ years of relative peace.
1868
Meiji Restoration — imperial rule restored, rapid modernization and industrialization begin.
1945
End of World War II; Japan adopts a new pacifist constitution in 1947.
1964
Tokyo hosts the Summer Olympics; Japan's "economic miracle" transforms it into a global power.
2019
Emperor Naruhito ascends the Chrysanthemum Throne, beginning the Reiwa era.
